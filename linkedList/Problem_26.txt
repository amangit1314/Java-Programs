//? Problem Title => Can we reverse a linked list in less than O(n) ?

//* Ans 
It doesnâ€™t look possible to reverse a simple singly linked list in less than O(n).
A simple singly linked list can only be reversed in O(n) time using recursive and iterative methods. 

A memory-efficient doubly linked list with head and tail pointers can also be reversed in O(1) time by swapping head and tail pointers. 

A doubly linked list with head and tail pointers can also be reversed in O(1) time by swapping head and tail pointers,
but we would have to traverse the list in forward direction using prev pointer and reverse direction using next pointer which may not be considered valid.
